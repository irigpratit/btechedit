About the Program

Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) has been one of the prominent sectors globally. Following Dewey's (1916) vision of TVET as a tool for education reform, the field continues to evolve and adapt to modern needs.

Contemporary scholars like Maclean & Wilson (2009) emphasize TVET's role in preparing individuals for gainful employment. The OECD (2017) further highlights how TVET programs equip young people with essential skills, knowledge, and competencies for professional careers.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum D+ grade in each subject of grade 12 with CGPA 1.8 or higher
  • Minimum second division marks in 10+2, PCL or equivalent in any discipline
  • Background knowledge of Information Technology or Computer Science

Career Opportunities

  • K-12 School IT Instructor
  • Educational Resource Material Developer
  • IT Curriculum Developer
  • Technical Education Specialist

Graduates can pursue advanced studies in:

  • Master's in TVET Programs
  • MTech Ed
  • Master's in Information Technology (with additional IT courses)
